# Deterministic Risk Scoring

The app now computes a deterministic score from findings so users can see
severity and confidence in a transparent way.

## Inputs

- `findings[]` entries generated by the analyzer.
- `initial_risk` from legacy rule-based logic (`safe|caution|danger`).

## Per-finding weights

- `safe`: +5
- `caution`: +14
- `danger`: +36

## Floors (to avoid contradictions)

- If `initial_risk == danger`, minimum score is `75`.
- If `initial_risk == caution`, minimum score is `40`.
- Safe scans have no floor — score reflects actual evidence only.

## Typosquatting / lookalike domains

Detected lookalike domains (e.g. `amaz0n.com`, `secure-paypal.com`) produce
**two** `danger` findings, pushing raw score to 72+ and confidence to `high`.

Generic DB-based typosquat checks are intentionally conservative to reduce
false positives (short/ambiguous labels are ignored, and only strong matches
to higher-traffic domains are escalated).

## Risk mapping

- `0-34`: `safe`
- `35-69`: `caution`
- `70-100`: `danger`

## Confidence mapping

- `high`:
  - score >= 85, or
  - at least two `danger` findings
- `medium`:
  - score >= 50, or
  - one `danger` finding, or
  - at least two `caution` findings
- `low` otherwise

## Why deterministic?

- Easy to explain to users and reviewers.
- Stable behavior across releases.
- Clear baseline before introducing optional model-based features.
